Welcome to Caveland Nature Park and Resort

History

In the foothills by Suhot Spring, covered by lush vegetation and overgrown trees, lies a network of subterranean caves, carved by water-a series of voids and chambers that sits on a solid limestone foundation and adorned with various types of stalactites, developed by the constant dripping of rainforest water. Thousands of years ago, water receded from the cave formations, revealing fossils of fresh water shells imbedded at the foothill along the walls of Suhot caves. The Firmaling family, owners of the estate, called the place "kaCuevaban", now known as Caveland Park.

DEPTH, DARKNESS, WONDER

CAVES

MAN CAVE



Man Cave is a spectacular refuge to visit and relax in privacy, gazing at the sky over the rock-forming bridge, supported by pillars and walls of stalactites and stalagmites.

CHOCOLATE CAVE



Chocolate Cave, its name was inspired by the color of the walls and stalactites nicely trickling.

RAINFOREST CAVE



Rainforest Cave, a shelter cave used by natives (Aeta) while hunting deer, wild pig, monkey and even water monitor Lizard (halo).

HOLY CAVE



Holy Cave, which houses a grotto that is shaped with a likeness to the Virgin Mary of Fatima, complete with a peaceful surrounding area that is suitable for praying and meditation.
